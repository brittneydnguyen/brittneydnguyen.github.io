Teaching

As an educator, I focus on the whole student. The capitalist society that seeks to optimize production trains students to think myopically, focusing solely on an end goal. However, as we learn, we go through all sorts of experiences that affect our personhood! This final exhibition & reflection activity is designed for students to identify how their experiences season their intellectual and emotional identities, while also showing off the skills they learned in class. Students also played a direct role in the creation of this project by answering a survey, which constituted the deidentified data for this activity!

In addition, I believe that humor plays a huge part in keeping students engaged, which is why I taught students joins in R using hypothetical hamsters.

Writing

If it wasn't evident enough, I am a rather colorful and maximalist writer! I love writing prose and poetry. Sometimes I also write scripts. You can find some selected works below. I don't formally publish any of these—I write mostly for my own enjoyment!

My affinity for science certainly seeps its way into my writing (and vice versa)! Dicot & Anthophila (2025) is a long-form poem about phenological mismatch, which I turned into a video for UCR's Artistic Expression of Original Research Program. Matter & Change (2024) is an essay on the second law of thermodynamics. Birdsong (2023) is a sci-fi story about habitat destruction and fascism, written as a final project for an environmental health class.

The natural world is a huge inspiration for me as well. The willow knows (2025) is a poem about the comfort and memory of nature. Another Day (2024) is a poem about the restorative power of springtime.

As with any former Catholic, spirituality plagues my writing. god? Are you out there? (2025) is a poem interrogating the many different forms god could occupy. /\/\/\/\/\ (2025) is a short story about waking up after a surgery. Ursa Major (2023) is a poem about constructing stories.

I can be grounded sometimes! Sometimes I write about people and places! Industry Cafe & Jazz (2025) is a poem about a little spot in Culver City of the same name. Your grandparents' old condo (2024) is concrete poem about a place I spent many Sundays in my childhood. Lately (2023) is a reflection on friendship. Home (2023 ed.) is a video project about my friends' ideas of a home.
